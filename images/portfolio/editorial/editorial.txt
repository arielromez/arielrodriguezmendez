a specialized branch of graphic design focused on the layout, typography, and visual arrangement of multi-page publications—both print and digital—such as magazines, newspapers, books, and blogs

Key Components of Editorial Design:
	Typography & Hierarchy: Selecting fonts and arranging them to guide the reader's attention from headlines to body text.
	Layout & Grids: Using structured frameworks to create consistent, organized pages, while managing white space to improve flow.
	Visual Content: Integrating images, illustrations, and infographics that support the narrative and enhance engagement.
	Pacing: Managing the rhythm of a publication, such as alternating long articles with image-heavy spreads. 

Platforms:
	Print: Magazines, newspapers, books, brochures.
	Digital: E-books, blogs, digital magazines, online news platforms. 
	
The goal of editorial design is to create a compelling visual experience that supports the editorial message, making complex information easier to digest.
