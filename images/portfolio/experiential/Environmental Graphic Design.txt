Experiential Graphic Design (EGD) is a specific subset focused on integrating graphic communication—like signage and typography—into the built environment to enhance user experience. 

Experiential Graphic Design (EGD) blends graphic design, architecture, and interior design to help our buildings’ users understand, navigate, and experience the built environment. EGD offers a high-impact tool for providing information, reinforcing brand identity, and assisting with wayfinding.

Key aspects of experiential design include:

	Environmental & Spatial Focus: It enhances how people navigate and feel within a space, using design to communicate information through signage, lighting, and layout.

	Sensory & Emotional Engagement: The design appeals to the senses (sight, sound, smell, touch, taste) to create an emotional connection and, sometimes, an "extraordinary" experience.
Narrative & Branding: It tells a story or reinforces a brand identity through interactive, high-impact tools that go beyond passive observation.

	Common Applications: Examples include museum exhibits, retail pop-up shops, corporate branding, public, and themed entertainment venues.
